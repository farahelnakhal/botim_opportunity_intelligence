# Value Proposition Template

One per segment × proposition. A value proposition passes only if it explains **organic** behaviour change — advertising, temporary discounts, unconnected cashback, and generic convenience do not count as switching reasons.

## 1. Header

- **Segment:** (from `knowledge-base/segments/` — Workstream A)
- **Decision-maker:** who actually decides (owner, finance manager, procurement)?
- **Job-to-be-done:** in the merchant's words.

## 2. Pain and status quo

- **Payment pain / credit pain:** severity, frequency, financial cost (cite evidence IDs).
- **Current workaround and its cost:** what they do today and what it costs them.
- **Existing alternatives and where they fail:** banks, BNPL, informal credit, other wallets.
- **Inflection point:** the moment the merchant becomes open to switching (rejection by bank, big order, VAT deadline, new outlet).

## 3. The proposition

One sentence: *For [segment] who [pain], [product] provides [benefit] unlike [alternative] because [BOTIM/AstraTech advantage].*

- **Organic switching reason** (pick the real one; must survive with all promotions removed): faster access to working capital / limits linked to actual activity / free business account / business IBAN / faster onboarding / automatic repayment / faster settlement / supplier discounts / lower fees / reduced cash handling / integrated accounting / easier supplier payments / better financial visibility / credit access unavailable elsewhere.
- **Quantified benefit:** AED/month or days saved for a typical merchant in this segment.
- **Payment behaviour required:** exactly what the merchant must route through BOTIM, and why they would.
- **Willingness to pay:** what they'd pay, based on what they pay today for worse.

## 4. The loop (if one exists)

Strong propositions compound:

> Payments generate data → data improves underwriting → credit drives payments → payments generate revenue → repayment improves credit access → the merchant relationship becomes more valuable over time.

State which links of this loop the proposition actually activates, and which are aspirational.

## 5. Honesty checks

- Remove all subsidies: does the merchant still move? Why?
- Is the benefit ≥10× the switching effort, or merely marginal?
- What would a sceptical merchant say? (Use real objections from evidence where available.)

## 6. Status

- Evidence confidence: High / Medium / Low
- Assumptions marked (A)
- Validation experiment(s) linked
- Classification from stress test

Store in `knowledge-base/product-ideas/<idea-slug>.md` (value-proposition section).
