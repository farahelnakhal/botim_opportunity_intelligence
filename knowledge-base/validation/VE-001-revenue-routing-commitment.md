# VE-001 — Revenue-Routing Commitment for Revenue-Linked Credit

- **Experiment ID:** VE-001
- **Proposition tested:** OPP-001 — Revenue-linked revolving credit on BOTIM business wallet (`opportunity-intelligence/test-cases/01-revenue-linked-revolving-credit.md`, backlog `BACKLOG.md`)
- **Hypothesis:** ≥40% of interviewed F&B/retail merchants (1–3 outlets) who report a working-capital gap in the last 90 days will complete a waitlist sign-up that requires them to state willingness to route ≥30% of incoming revenue through a BOTIM business wallet in exchange for a credit limit that grows with that routed revenue.
- **Target participants:** owner-operators of F&B/retail businesses, 1–3 outlets, AED 50k–300k monthly revenue, Dubai/Sharjah.
- **Recruitment criteria:**
  - Include: trade licence holder or owner-operator; business ≥12 months old; experienced a working-capital gap in the last 90 days (screened in stage 1).
  - Exclude: merchants with an existing AstraTech lending relationship; personal contacts of the team; anyone recruited via a BOTIM promotional channel (avoids enthusiast bias); businesses >3 outlets.
- **Method:** two stages.
  - **Stage 1 — interviews (past behaviour first):** 15 structured interviews using the guide below. No product concept is shown until section C.
  - **Stage 2 — commitment proxy:** at interview end, and separately to a wider recruited sample, offer a real waitlist form. The form states the routing condition explicitly ("your limit grows with revenue received into the wallet; we expect at least ~30% of your incoming payments to flow through it") and requires trade-licence number and preferred monthly limit — a small but real cost to sign.
- **Sample size:** 15 interviews (enough to saturate qualitative workaround patterns) + minimum 50 waitlist offers (distinguishes 40% success from 15% failure threshold with a usable margin at this scale). One-line justification: this is a directional kill-test, not an estimation study.
- **Success threshold (pre-committed):** ≥40% of qualified merchants offered the waitlist complete it including the routing condition; AND ≥8 of 15 interviewees describe a costly current workaround (informal credit, personal cards, missed stock) without prompting.
- **Failure threshold (pre-committed):** ≤15% waitlist completion, OR ≥8 of 15 interviewees show no costly workaround. Result between thresholds → inconclusive: redesign incentive or segment, do not extend silently.
- **Duration:** 3 weeks from first interview; hard stop.
- **Data collected:** screening answers, verbatims (tagged for hand-off to Workstream A via REQ queue), workaround type and estimated monthly cost, waitlist completion rate, stated routing willingness, preferred limit. Stored in this folder; personal data minimised to name/contact/licence no. with consent line on the form.
- **Decision informed:** whether OPP-001 stays "Promising but unvalidated" and proceeds to a 7-week concierge MVP design, or is reclassified Weak (routing condition kills conversion). Decision owner: Workstream B, presented via meeting-ready output.

## Interview guide (non-leading; past behaviour before concept)

**A. Money-in today:** How do customers pay you — roughly what split between cash, card, transfers, wallets? Where does that money land?
**B. The last gap:** Tell me about the last time cash was tight. What caused it? How did you cover it? What did that cost you (fees, interest, favours, missed stock)? How often does this happen? What have you tried before — bank loan, supplier terms, anything else? What happened?
**C. Concept (shown last, neutrally):** A business wallet where a credit limit grows automatically with the revenue you receive into it; repayment is taken as a small percentage of incoming payments. What's your first reaction? What would worry you about it? What share of your incoming payments could realistically arrive into a wallet like this — and what stops the rest?
**D. Close:** waitlist offer (commitment proxy), no persuasion beyond reading the form.

Anti-leading rules applied: no "would you use…?" as a signal; concept order fixed last; objections explicitly invited; interest without cost is not counted.
