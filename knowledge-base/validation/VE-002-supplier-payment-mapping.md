# VE-002 — Supplier-Payment Mapping and Card-Acceptance Willingness

- **Experiment ID:** VE-002
- **Proposition tested:** OPP-002 — Supplier-payment commercial card with free-credit days (`opportunity-intelligence/test-cases/02-supplier-payment-card.md`, backlog `BACKLOG.md`). Results also feed OPP-006 (inventory financing wallet).
- **Hypothesis:** ≥20% of the supplier relationships named by target merchants are payable by commercial card today or the supplier states willingness to accept card **without surcharging**, covering ≥30% of the merchants' monthly supplier spend by value.
- **Target participants:**
  - Merchants: trading/retail SMEs, AED 30k–150k/month supplier spend, UAE.
  - Suppliers: the actual suppliers those merchants name (wholesalers, distributors, importers).
- **Recruitment criteria:**
  - Merchants — include: owner or person who pays suppliers; ≥3 active supplier relationships. Exclude: AstraTech borrowers, team contacts, franchisees whose supply is centrally invoiced (payment terms not theirs to change).
  - Suppliers — include only suppliers named by interviewed merchants (real chains, not a convenience sample of card-friendly suppliers).
- **Method:** internal-data-free desk-plus-field mapping in two stages.
  - **Stage 1 — merchant interviews (12):** map each merchant's top 5 suppliers: instrument used (cash / transfer / cheque / card), payment terms (days), any surcharge experience, who sets the terms. Past behaviour only; no product concept shown — this experiment tests the rail, not the offer.
  - **Stage 2 — supplier calls (10):** for named suppliers: do you accept card today? At what cost to you (MDR awareness)? Would you accept card from a good customer without surcharge? What would make you refuse?
- **Sample size:** 12 merchants (≈60 supplier relationships mapped) + 10 supplier calls. Justification: enough relationships to estimate the acceptance share against a 20% threshold; supplier calls verify merchant reports rather than sizing the market.
- **Success threshold (pre-committed):** ≥20% of mapped supplier relationships card-payable without surcharge AND those relationships cover ≥30% of supplier spend by value.
- **Failure threshold (pre-committed):** <20% of relationships card-payable without surcharge → **reclassify OPP-002 as Weak** in the backlog (as pre-committed in the test case). Between thresholds on the two conditions → inconclusive: extend supplier calls by 10 before deciding.
- **Duration:** 3 weeks; hard stop.
- **Data collected:** supplier-payment map per merchant (instrument, terms, value share), surcharge incidence, supplier acceptance answers with stated reasons. Stored in this folder; supplier names pseudonymised in shared outputs. Verbatims tagged for Workstream A hand-off (fulfils part of REQ-003 from our side; primary evidence collection remains theirs).
- **Decision informed:** whether OPP-002's commercial model gets built out to full `commercial-model.md` depth and proceeds toward MVP design, or is reclassified Weak with the acceptance data as the decisive record. Also sets the supplier-side feasibility input for OPP-006. Decision owner: Workstream B.

## Question-design notes

- Merchants are asked how they *paid last month*, supplier by supplier — not whether they'd "like to pay by card".
- Supplier willingness is tested against a named good customer, not an abstract scheme, and the surcharge question is asked before any mention of BOTIM.
- No free-credit-days concept is shown to either side; benefit framing would contaminate the rail measurement.
