### Gaps & implications

- **Gaps:** merchant acquiring only now being built (Pine Labs, payments co. 2026) — acceptance side historically absent; freeze/compliance handling opaque (no written policies cited by customers, no phone escalation); complex-structure onboarding weak (offshore parents, non-UAE-SIM founders); trade finance absent; cards AED-only (2% FX on everything else); Saving Spaces rate ambiguity (KFS 1% vs site "up to 3%/3.5%" — could not verify).
- **Implication for BOTIM (inference):** Wio is the reference threat — a funded, profitable, government-linked digital bank moving from SME deposits into exactly the merchant-payments + SME-credit territory BOTIM is exploring, and distributing through merchant networks (Geidea) rather than direct only. Time sensitivity captured in IP-2026-001. Its published credit pricing sets the bar to beat: 12% p.a. on a 90-day POS-swept line is the "good" price; 41–55% APR on card/quick-cash revolving is the expensive tail. Transaction-linked repayment (daily POS sweep) is already live in-market — BOTIM's revenue-linked repayment concept would not be first, but BOTIM's consumer reach + merchant relationships remain differential vs Wio's licence/balance sheet. Wio's freeze-opacity and edge-case onboarding failures mirror the PSP pain pattern — service quality in the tail is an open flank even against the best-funded player.
